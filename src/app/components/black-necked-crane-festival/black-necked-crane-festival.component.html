Black Necked Crane Festival

The black-necked crane festivals are celebrated every year on 11 November, coinciding with the birth anniversary of His Majesty the fourth King Jigme Singye Wangchuck. It is held in the courtyards of Gangtey Monastery and marks the return of the majestic birds. The community considers black-necked cranes as heavenly birds that are integral to the holy land. The magnificent birds circle the Gangtey monastery every year on their arrival and even on their departure. It has become a custom for the local students to perform crane dances in costumes yearly. Also, monks perform the famous Dramitse Ngacham (the Drums of Dramitse), one of UNESCO's tangible heritage dances from Bhutan.

It is an important community event where the local community comes together to renew and strengthen its commitment to conserving the majestic Black Necked Crane and the rich culture and tradition that prospers.
