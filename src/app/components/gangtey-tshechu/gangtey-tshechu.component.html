Gangtey Tshechu

Tshechu is a religious festival of mask dances performed by monks and laymen in tribute to Guru Rinpoche. The lotus-born master is the most prominent figure in Tibetan Buddhism. Guru Rinpoche is said to have brought the first teachings of Buddhism to the country and fundamentally changed the way Bhutanese people lived.

The Gangtey Monastery, also called the Gangtey Sangngak Chöling, was established by Gangtey Truelku Pema Thinley following Pema Lingpa’s prophecies. Pema Lingpa is the most revered and celebrated saint of Bhutanese origin. It is said that all the choreographic instructions for the mask dances were imparted to Pema Lingpa in his meditative states by Guru Rinpoche. These treasure mask dances are neither of mere origin nor of ordinary meaning. It is a wondrous transmission of teachings through expressions of our body, mind, and speech. The mask dances are believed to cleanse the sins and bring the viewers prosperity, peace, and joy. These sacred dances are performed in the courtyards of Gangtey Goenpa monastery for three consecutive days.

Gangtey Tshechu is a great time of celebration and gathering for the people residing in the nearby villages. Villagers and people from all works of life gather in fully adorned jewels, hand-woven dresses, and the brightest smiles. Families and friends gather to share the most delicious and incredible of foods and, most importantly, each other’s company. People pray and wish for all their loved ones to gather as same in the following years Tshechu in good health and wealth.
